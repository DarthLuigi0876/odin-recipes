Doughnut

Description

A doughnut (sometimes spelt donut in American English; both is a type of pastry made from leavened fried dough. It is popular in many countries and is prepared in various forms as a sweet snack that can be homemade or purchased in bakeries, supermarkets, food stalls, and franchised specialty vendors. Doughnut is the traditional spelling, while donut is the simplified version; the terms are used interchangeably.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1/4 cup milk
  • 1/4 cup sour cream
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 350Â°F. Grease a doughnut pan.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  3. In a medium bowl, whisk together milk, sour cream, butter, egg, and vanilla extract.
  4. Add wet ingredients to dry ingredients and stir until just combined.
  5. Transfer batter to a piping bag or a ziplock bag with the corner snipped off and pipe into doughnut pan.
  6. Bake for 10-12 minutes, or until doughnuts spring back when touched.
  7. Let cool in pan for 5 minutes, then transfer to a wire rack to cool completely.
